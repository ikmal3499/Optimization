Palm Oil

Palm oil is a dominant force in the global vegetable oil market, with an annual production and consumption of around 45.3 million tonnes. It was first introduced to Malaysia in 1870 and has significantly contributed to the country's economy. However, the industry faced challenges during the COVID-19 pandemic, as infections surged, border closures disrupted labor supply, and a shortage of fruit on the market led to historically high Crude Palm Oil (CPO) prices. While these price increases benefited export earnings, billions of ringgits were lost due to unharvested fruit bunches, largely offsetting the financial gains for the Malaysian palm oil business.



Optimization Route

Optimal transportation of Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) is crucial for achieving transportation objectives, which include quality, quantity, place, time, and cost. Delay in the transportation of Fresh Fruit Bunches (FFB) to the Palm Oil Factory (PKS) can lead to poor quality FFA. Therefore, transportation delays can significantly impact the quality of FFA. One of the reasons for the FFA is overripe fruit, which is caused by poor handling and field operations that lead to delays in transportation.
